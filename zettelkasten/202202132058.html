[DRAFT] Derivative

#maths

The derivative of f at the point x is

lim(f(x+h) - f(x)) / h where h->0 or

lim(f(w) - f(x)) / (w - x) where w->x.

If this limit exists, then we can say that f is differentiable at point x and the derivative is shown as f'(x)(f-prime) or d/dxf(x). The derivative is a ratio.
